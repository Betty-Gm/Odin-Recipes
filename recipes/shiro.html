Shiro-Wet

Shiro Wat is a beloved Ethiopian vegetarian stew made from seasoned chickpea flour, simmered into a smooth, thick, and richly spiced sauce. It's a staple comfort food in Ethiopian households, especially popular during fasting periods, and pairs perfectly with injera.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup shiro powder (spiced chickpea flour)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, minced
  • 2 tbsp niter kibbeh or vegetable oil
  • 1-2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tsp berbere spice (optional, adjust to taste)
  • 3 cups water
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Heat niter kibbeh or oil in a pot; sauté onions until translucent.
  2. Add garlic and ginger; cook for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes and berbere spice; cook until softened.
  4. Slowly whisk in shiro powder with water to avoid lumps (can mix shiro with a bit of water first to form a smooth paste).
  5. Pour the mixture into the pot, stirring constantly.
  6. Simmer on low heat for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until thickened and smooth.
  7. Season with salt to taste.
  8. Serve hot with injera.
