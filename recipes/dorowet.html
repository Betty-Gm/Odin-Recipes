Doro-Wet (Ethiopian Chicken Stew)

Doro Wat is Ethiopia's national dish — a deeply spiced chicken stew simmered in a rich, fiery berbere sauce with caramelized onions and hard-boiled eggs. It's traditionally served with injera and is a centerpiece dish at Ethiopian celebrations and gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 4 large onions, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup berbere spice
  • 1/4 cup niter kibbeh (Ethiopian spiced butter) or regular butter
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, minced
  • 4 hard-boiled eggs
  • 2 lemons (for cleaning chicken)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup water or chicken broth

Steps

  1. Clean chicken pieces with lemon juice and water; set aside.
  2. In a large pot, cook chopped onions over medium heat, stirring frequently, until deeply golden (about 20-25 minutes) — no oil needed at first, let them release their own moisture.
  3. Add niter kibbeh, garlic, and ginger; cook 2-3 minutes.
  4. Stir in berbere spice; cook another few minutes to release the flavors.
  5. Add water/broth and bring to a simmer.
  6. Add chicken pieces, cover, and simmer 30-40 minutes until chicken is tender.
  7. Add hard-boiled eggs in the last 10 minutes so they soak up the sauce.
  8. Season with salt to taste, and serve hot with injera.
